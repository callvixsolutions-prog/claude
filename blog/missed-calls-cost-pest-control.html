What missed calls really cost a pest control business

Callvix Solutions6 min readUpdated July 2026

Every pest control owner knows the feeling: you get out of a crawl space, check your phone, and see two missed calls. You call back — no answer. They already booked someone else. Missed calls aren't a minor annoyance; for a pest control business, they're one of the biggest silent leaks in your revenue. Here's how to put a real number on it.

The short version: Industry research suggests roughly 85% of callers who hit voicemail never call back, and about 80% won't leave a voicemail at all during an urgent issue. So most missed calls are simply lost. With a few numbers from your own business, you can estimate exactly how much that's costing you each month.

Why pest control loses more than most trades

Two things make missed calls especially expensive in pest control:

  • Urgency. Wasps by the door, rodents in the attic, ants across the kitchen — people want it handled today. If you don't answer, the next company will.
  • Recurring value. A one-time call often becomes a quarterly plan. Losing the first call means losing the whole relationship, not just one job.

Research across home-service categories also shows that leads answered within five minutes convert at 5–10× the rate of leads followed up an hour later. In pest control, the window is even shorter — often just the time it takes to dial the next result on Google.

The simple missed-call math

You can estimate your own lost revenue with three numbers:

  • A = missed calls per month (check your phone or call logs)
  • B = your booking rate on answered calls (e.g. 40%)
  • C = average value of a first job (e.g. $300–$500)

Your rough monthly lost revenue is simply A × B × C.

A worked example

InputValue
Missed calls per month (A)40
Booking rate (B)40%
Average first job (C)$500
Lost first-job revenue / month≈ $8,000
Lost revenue / year≈ $96,000

And that only counts the first job. Because many of those callers would have become recurring quarterly accounts worth $600–$1,200 a year each, the true lifetime cost is considerably higher.

Where the calls are actually being missed

For most pest control companies, missed calls cluster in three predictable gaps:

  • During service hours, when you and your techs are on-site and can't get to the phone.
  • After hours and weekends, when the office is closed but pest problems aren't.
  • During busy-season overflow, when calls come in faster than one person can answer.

How to plug the leak

The fix isn't "try to answer faster" — you're already busy running jobs. It's making sure a capable person is always there to pick up, qualify the issue, and book the appointment on your behalf. That's exactly what a pest control answering service does: your phone is covered in your company name whether you're in a crawl space, driving, or closed for the night.

Frequently asked questions

How many pest control leads are lost to missed calls?

Industry research suggests around 85% of callers who reach voicemail never call back, and roughly 80% won't leave a voicemail during an urgent issue — so most missed calls are lost outright.

How do I calculate the cost of missed calls?

Multiply monthly missed calls × your booking rate × the average value of a job. For example, 40 × 40% × $500 ≈ $8,000/month in lost first-job revenue, before counting recurring accounts.

Why do so many callers not leave a voicemail?

Pest problems feel urgent. Homeowners want help now, not a callback later, so if no one answers they immediately try the next company.
